Act 3: So I found...



Three-act narrative structure

  • The problem was the most salient narrative element in visual stories

  • Space-based characters were relatively successful

  • The timeframe was not discussed more in the responses

  • A bias towards effects compared to causes during story comprehension












Story genre

  • Higher retention accuracy rate with longform infographics than dynamic slideshows, especially in identify (numerical)

    • Total: 75.9% v.s. 70.8%; numerical: 71.0% v.s. 60.6%

  • Better comprehension with longform infographics than dynamic slideshows, especially in problem

    • Total: 49.4% v.s. 46.6%; Problem: 99.2% v.s. 92.7%









Story trope

  • Higher retention accuracy with leader line than color highlighting

    • Total: 75.0% v.s. 69.1%

  • Leader line helped readers compare key characters (i.e., understand the tension) in the story better than color highlighting

    • Tension: 73.6% v.s. 64.8%









Individual difference

  • Positive feedback on genres and tropes

  • Interests and familiarity with internet and online resource have positive influence on retention

  • Concerns and beliefs have influences on story affections(A: bored--; interested, concern, agreed++; B: change of beliefs)






















Epilogue: Design recommendations



  • Use three-act narrative structure for your story

  • Design map-based story in longform infographics

  • Use leader line for addressing key points

  • Intrigue interests
